Trivandrum, colloquially known as Thiruvananthapuram with a rather local spin on the pronunciation, is the capital of Kerala, and the largest city in the state. With a population of 1.68 million, Trivandrum holds the highest population count in the state and whilst being a major Information Technology hub integrated with the most developed infrastructure, Trivandrum is the fifth most populous urban agglomeration in Kerala. Trivandrum alongside its urbanization success is also one of the most excelling cities in terms of academic flourish, space science, research and technology, religious practices, Malayalam cinema, pleasant climatic conditions and defence headquarters.

Trivandrum built on the seven hills and along the seashore on the west coast is a true specimen of biodiversity in the Western Ghats, where the charm of romantic beaches meets the thrill of adventurous hills. Mesmerized by the natural beauty of Trivandrum, Mahatma Gandhi referred to this city as the Evergreen City of India. Trivandrum not only seduces the visitors with its flora and fauna glamour but also with its rich possession of historical significance. The religious and heritage monuments of this city are a striking reflection of architectural brilliance of ancient Dravidian empires.
